About This Game:

The Game of Life is a cellular-automaton, zero player game, developed by John Conway in 1970.


Based on the state of each cell, whether each cell is alive or dead through a given round of the game, the community of cells evolve.


The rules are as follow:

◮ Birth: a dead cell will come alive if exactly 3 of its neighbors were alive.


◮ Death: a cell can die by:

◮ Overcrowding: if an alive cell has 3 or more alive neighbors, the cell will die.

◮ Exposure: If an alive cell has only 1 or 0 live neighbors, it will die


◮ Survival: a cell survives round to round if and only if 2 or 3 of its neighbors are alive.


Pay attention and see repetitive types of “life-forms” created in the game and whether a game of life session will go on infinitely.
